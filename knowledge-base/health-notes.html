Health Notes

Guidelines for Gout and Fatty Liver management. These inform every recipe rating and meal plan decision.

Gout

Gout is caused by elevated uric acid (hyperuricemia), which crystallizes in joints. Dietary management focuses on reducing purine intake, staying hydrated, and avoiding known triggers.

High-purine foods to avoid

  • Organ meats (liver, kidneys, sweetbreads)
  • Most shellfish (mussels, scallops, sardines, anchovies)
  • Alcohol — especially beer and spirits
  • High-fructose corn syrup (sodas, sweetened drinks)
  • Excessive red meat daily

Moderate-purine foods — limit frequency

  • Shrimp — max 2x/week
  • Ground beef and red meat — 2–3x/week max
  • Chicken — lower purine than red meat, fine daily
  • Eggs — low purine, fine daily in moderation

Gout-supportive foods

  • Tart cherry juice — 4–6 oz/day diluted (lowers uric acid)
  • Lemon water — alkalizes urine, aids uric acid flushing
  • Plain water — primary tool, stay well hydrated
  • Low-fat dairy — may reduce uric acid
  • Complex carbs: oats, brown rice, vegetables

Fatty Liver

Fatty liver (NAFLD) is managed through diet and weight loss. The goal is reducing liver fat by minimizing dietary fat load, added sugars, and refined carbohydrates, while supporting weight loss overall.

Foods to minimize

  • Saturated fat — fatty cuts of meat, full-fat dairy, butter
  • Added sugars and sweetened beverages
  • Refined carbohydrates — white bread, white rice in excess
  • Alcohol — even small amounts stress the liver

Fatty liver-supportive choices

  • Lean proteins: boneless skinless chicken, 90/10 ground beef, eggs, shrimp
  • High-fiber foods: oats, vegetables, legumes
  • Healthy fats: olive oil (replace butter and lard)
  • Brown rice or cauliflower rice over white rice
  • Plain Greek yogurt over flavored

Weight goal

Target: lose ~25 lbs (10% of ~250 lb starting weight)
  • This is medically urgent per physician guidance.
  • Daily calorie target: 1,800–2,000 cal (moderate deficit)
  • Meal plan structure supports this without aggressive restriction
  • Snack portions are the most variable — track carefully
  • Weight loss itself is the single biggest lever for improving both Gout and Fatty Liver outcomes
