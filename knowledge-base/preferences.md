# Preferences & Guidelines

This document defines the rules and conventions for all recipes and meal plans in this system.

---

## Dietary Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Avoid** | Onions, tomatoes (in most recipes) |
| **No turkey** | Not a preference |
| **Seafood** | Most seafood avoided — shrimp is the exception |
| **Shrimp limit** | Max 2x per week (moderate purines) |
| **Red meat** | Use 90/10 ground beef strictly for Fatty Liver management |
| **Sweeteners** | No high-fructose corn syrup. Honey or maple syrup in small amounts only |
| **Yogurt** | Plain only — no flavored/added-sugar varieties |
| **Rice** | Brown rice or cauliflower rice preferred over white for at least half servings |

## Equipment

**Primary: Ninja Crispi Pro (4-quart basket)**

| Setting | Use |
|---------|-----|
| 400°F | Shrimp (8–10 min), Chicken initial high heat |
| 380°F | Boneless skinless chicken thighs (18–22 min, flip halfway) |
| 375°F | Roasted veggies (18–20 min), Reheat (5–8 min) |

**Reheat standard:** 375°F for 5–8 minutes, shake halfway through.

## Rating System

All recipes are rated on two health axes:

### Gout-Friendly (1–5 ⭐)
Based on purine content and known gout dietary triggers.

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ★★★★★ | Very low purine, no known triggers |
| ★★★★☆ | Low purine, minor considerations |
| ★★★☆☆ | Moderate purine — limit frequency |
| ★★☆☆☆ | High purine — use sparingly |
| ★☆☆☆☆ | Avoid — significant gout risk |

Known triggers to avoid: organ meats, shellfish (except shrimp in moderation), alcohol, high-fructose corn syrup, excessive red meat.

### Fatty Liver-Friendly (1–5 ⭐)
Based on saturated fat, added sugars, refined carbohydrates, and alcohol content.

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ★★★★★ | Ideal — minimal sat fat, sugar, refined carbs |
| ★★★★☆ | Good — minor areas to watch |
| ★★★☆☆ | Moderate — use with portion awareness |
| ★★☆☆☆ | Use sparingly |
| ★☆☆☆☆ | Avoid |
