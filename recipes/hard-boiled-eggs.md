# Hard Boiled Eggs

**Serves:** 4 | **Serving Size:** 2 eggs | **Cook:** Stovetop

| Gout-Friendly | Fatty Liver-Friendly |
|---------------|----------------------|
| ★★★★☆ (4/5) | ★★★★☆ (4/5) |

Eggs are low-purine. Yolks are fine in moderation — limit to 1–2 servings per day given cholesterol. Nutrient-dense, moderate saturated fat, well within reasonable range at 2 eggs/day.

---

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs
- Water to cover
- Ice for ice bath

---

## Instructions

1. Place eggs in a single layer in a saucepan. Cover with cold water by 1 inch.
2. Bring to a full boil over medium-high heat.
3. Once boiling, remove from heat, cover with a lid, and let sit for exactly **11 minutes** for fully set yolks with no gray ring.
4. While eggs sit, fill a large bowl with ice and cold water.
5. Transfer eggs immediately to the ice bath. Let cool for at least 10 minutes.
6. Peel under cool running water. Store peeled or unpeeled.

## Meal Prep Notes

- **Unpeeled:** Keep in fridge for up to **1 week**.
- **Peeled:** Keep in fridge for up to **5 days** in a container covered with water.
- The ice bath is not optional — it stops the cooking and prevents the green ring on the yolk.
- Use as a snack, slice over roasted veggies, or chop into a scramble.
