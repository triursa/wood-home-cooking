<h2>Ingredients</h2>
<ul class="ingredients-list">
  <li>8 large eggs</li>
  <li>Water to cover by 1 inch</li>
  <li>Ice + cold water for ice bath</li>
</ul>

<h2>Instructions</h2>
<ol class="steps-list">
  <li>Place eggs in a single layer in a saucepan. Cover with cold water by 1 inch.</li>
  <li>Bring to a full boil over medium-high heat.</li>
  <li>Once boiling, remove from heat, cover with a lid, and let sit for exactly <strong>11 minutes</strong> for fully set yolks with no gray ring.</li>
  <li>While eggs sit, fill a large bowl with ice and cold water.</li>
  <li>Transfer eggs immediately to the ice bath. Let cool at least 10 minutes.</li>
  <li>Peel under cool running water. Store peeled or unpeeled.</li>
</ol>

<div class="notes-box">
  <div class="notes-title">Meal Prep Notes</div>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Unpeeled:</strong> Keep in fridge up to <strong>1 week</strong>.</li>
    <li><strong>Peeled:</strong> Keep in fridge up to <strong>5 days</strong> in a container covered with water.</li>
    <li>The ice bath is not optional — it stops the cooking and prevents the green ring on the yolk.</li>
    <li>Use as a snack, slice over roasted veggies, or chop into a scramble.</li>
  </ul>
</div>
